# Set design translation

## Start with the camera, not the objects

Lock the camera crop and subject position before adding props. The main visual outcome is driven by the relationship between face, camera, and the depth path behind the creator.

## Main light patterns

| Situation | Starting pattern | Guardrail |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Soft, credible talking head | large diffused key 35–45° from camera axis, slightly above eye line | place on the flattering face side, not automatically on a room side |
| Dark curtain / moody tech look | key on open/room side; curtain side stays 1–2 stops darker | do not let the curtain become a flat black void |
| Warm shelf / plant background | neutral face key, warm practical in background 1–2 stops below face | practical decorates the scene; it is not the key light |
| Black or charcoal shirt | add modest rim or a brighter background edge | preserve texture and shoulder separation |

## Practical staging hierarchy

Use a maximum of three background story layers:

1. **Anchor:** one shelf, framed art, or wall plane.
2. **Organic contrast:** one substantial plant or sculptural form.
3. **Proof of work:** restrained books, a camera, microphone, notebook, or compact creator tool.

Keep focal objects around head/shoulder height or clearly on a shelf. Avoid random low-value objects, fake laptops, active displays, RGB strips, and visible storage clutter.

## AI/tech creator direction

- Palette: dark neutral shirt, gray/charcoal curtain or wall, walnut/black hardware, a single warm practical, controlled green from one plant.
- Tone: useful, calm, precise; closer to a product designer's working set than a gaming setup.
- Background: give the eye one readable destination, then leave negative space for captions and cuts.

## Diagram requirement

Draw a top-down plan before the final instructions. Show the fixed room planes, desk, camera, person, key, fill/negative fill, practical/background light, and the actual depth path. Use the coordinate convention from `spatial-reading.md`.
