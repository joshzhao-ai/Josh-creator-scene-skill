# Spatial reading

## Coordinate convention

Use the final recorded image as the primary reference frame.

- **画面左/右**: viewer's left/right in the final video.
- **人物左/右**: the creator's own left/right while seated or standing.
- **相机左/右**: left/right while standing behind the camera and looking at the creator.

Always pair the first two when positioning visible key or fill light: `画面右（人物左）前方 35–45°`. If camera and subject are angled, add the relation in words instead of assuming they are mirrored.

## Read the scene in planes

| Plane | Identify | Use |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Foreground | desk edge, lens side, crop intrusions | keep clean; include only intentional work cues |
| Subject | chair, eye line, body turn, hand area | establish authority and comfort |
| Midground | wall, curtain, shelf, plant, practical light | create separation and one readable story |
| Background depth | receding wall/corner/window/door | preserve an existing triangle or leading line; do not invent one |

## Minimum spatial read

1. Mark camera position and whether it is front-on or three-quarter.
2. Mark the subject's body angle separately from the desk angle.
3. Trace the actual visible background path from chair to wall/corner/window.
4. Identify fixed elements: desk, curtain, window, door, built-ins, radiator, large appliances.
5. Identify removable visual noise and likely power/daylight controls.
6. State the confidence level: `confirmed from photo`, `likely`, or `needs sweep video`.

## Geometry guardrails

- Keep a straight desk straight in any diagram/render unless the photo proves otherwise.
- Do not turn an open background area into a physical triangle prop. A "triangle" may be a camera-view depth relationship.
- Do not place a background light where it would be visible in frame unless it is intended as a practical.
- Keep shelves against a real wall. Never add an unsupported side table or a screen simply to signal "technology".
